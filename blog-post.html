Getting your first listing ready for guests

12 March 2026

Your first short-term rental listing sets the tone for every booking. Taking time to prepare the property, photos, and listing copy pays off in better reviews and fewer support headaches.

Start with a deep clean and a neutral, welcoming style. Remove personal items and clutter so guests feel they have their own space. Invest in quality bedding and towels—guests notice. A small welcome guide (house rules, WiFi, local tips) goes a long way.

Checklist before you go live

Before you publish, make sure you have: professional or high-quality photos, a clear description of the space and amenities, accurate check-in and check-out instructions, and a plan for who handles cleaning and key handover. If you’re using a management partner like Airoam, they can handle messaging and operations so you can focus on the property itself.

After the first few bookings

Use early reviews to refine your listing and address any recurring feedback. Small improvements—extra pillows, better lighting, a coffee machine—often show up in ratings. Once your first listing runs smoothly, you’ll have a repeatable playbook for scaling to more properties.
