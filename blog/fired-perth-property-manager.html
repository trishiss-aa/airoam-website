Fired My Perth Property Manager After 6 Months. Here's What I Learned.

Hidden fees, 4–6 hour response times, and a maintenance issue I only discovered in a review. What went wrong - and what I wish I'd known before signing up.

When I bought my investment property in Scarborough three years ago, hiring a property manager seemed like the obvious move. I had a demanding job, zero experience with short-term rentals, and the agency promised 'full-service management' that would handle everything. Six months later, I fired them. Here's what went wrong - and what I wish I'd known from the start.

The Promise vs. The Reality

The pitch was compelling: 'We'll maximise your returns while you sit back and collect the income.' For 18% of my rental revenue, they'd handle listings, guest communication, cleaning coordination, maintenance, and pricing. I signed up expecting a passive investment. What I got was a part-time job I hadn't applied for.

Within the first month, I noticed response times to guest enquiries averaging 4–6 hours. Potential guests were booking elsewhere. When I raised it, I was told their team was 'stretched' and they'd 'try to improve.' They didn't.

Then came the pricing issues. My property sat empty during what turned out to be a busy weekend because the rate was 40% higher than comparable listings. 'We use market data,' they assured me. Apparently not very well.

The Hidden Costs They Don't Mention

The 18% headline fee was just the beginning. There were 'admin fees' for booking modifications. Charges for 'emergency coordination' when a guest locked themselves out (on a Saturday, naturally). A markup on cleaning that I only discovered when I compared quotes directly with the cleaners.

By month four, I calculated my actual costs at closer to 24% of revenue. And my revenue was already lower than it should have been due to poor pricing and slow responses.

The final straw was a maintenance issue that went unreported for three weeks. A guest had mentioned a slow-draining shower in their review - the first I'd heard of it. When I asked why I wasn't informed, the manager said they'd 'missed it in the review monitoring.' Three weeks of guests dealing with a drain issue, and potentially telling friends never to book my place.

What I Needed (But Didn't Know To Ask For)

Looking back, my mistake was assuming all property managers deliver roughly the same service. They don't. The gap between good and bad management is enormous - and the gap between traditional management and AI-powered systems is even larger.

Here's what actually matters for a Perth short-term rental:

Instant response times. Not 'within business hours' - instant. Guests expect immediate answers, and the platforms reward hosts who provide them. If your manager can't guarantee sub-30-minute responses around the clock, you're losing bookings.

Dynamic pricing that actually works. Perth's market varies significantly by season, events, and even day of week. Your pricing should adjust automatically based on real-time data, not monthly reviews by an overworked analyst.

Proactive maintenance detection. Waiting for guests to complain - or worse, leave bad reviews - is reactive management. The system should identify potential issues before they impact guests.

Transparent reporting. I should see my performance data in real-time: occupancy, revenue, guest ratings, comparison to market benchmarks. If your manager sends monthly PDFs and calls that 'reporting,' you're being kept in the dark.

The Switch That Changed Everything

When I moved my Scarborough property to Airoam, the difference was immediate. The onboarding process was thorough - professional photography, listing optimisation, integration with all major platforms. But what really stood out was the technology.

Their AI handles guest messages instantly, 24/7. The pricing adjusts multiple times daily based on demand signals I'd never have time to monitor. Their maintenance detection flagged a potential hot water issue from a guest comment I wouldn't have even noticed - and had it checked before the next arrival.

Most importantly, I actually get to be hands-off. The Owner Insights Dashboard shows me everything I need to know at a glance, and their team reaches out proactively when decisions need my input. It's what 'full-service management' should have been all along.

The Numbers Don't Lie

In my first full quarter with Airoam, my Scarborough property earned 28% more than the same period under my previous manager. My occupancy increased, my average nightly rate increased, and I paid 9% in fees instead of 18% (plus hidden costs).

That's not a testimonial I was paid to give. It's just maths.

Questions to Ask Your Current Manager

If you're working with a Perth property manager and wondering whether you're getting good value, here's what I'd ask:

  • •What's your average response time to guest enquiries? (If they can't answer precisely, they're not tracking it.)
  • •How often does pricing adjust, and what data drives those changes?
  • •How do you identify maintenance issues before guests complain?
  • •Can I see real-time performance data, or do I wait for monthly reports?
  • •What's my all-in cost, including every fee and markup?

Pro Tip

Ask for a written breakdown of every fee before you sign. Admin fees, coordination charges, cleaning markups, and 'extras' can push your real cost to 22–25% of revenue. Transparent 9% with no hidden add-ons is a different equation.
