Dynamic pricing can boost your Airbnb revenue by 15 to 25%. The good news: you can set it up yourself with free or cheap tools. The honest truth: most hosts abandon DIY pricing within three months because the time investment outweighs the gains. Here's exactly how to do it yourself, what it actually costs in hours, and how to decide if it's worth it.

Why Dynamic Pricing Matters

Static pricing is leaving money on the table. If you charge $200 per night year round, you're undercharging during the Australian Open and overcharging on quiet winter Tuesdays. The result: missed revenue during peaks and empty nights during troughs.

Dynamic pricing adjusts your rates based on demand, events, seasonality, day of week, competitor pricing, and booking pace. Done well, it captures maximum revenue without sacrificing occupancy.

The Free and Cheap Tools Available

Option 1: Airbnb Smart Pricing (Free)

Airbnb's built-in Smart Pricing requires zero setup. You set a minimum and maximum price, and Airbnb adjusts within that range.

  • •Pros: Completely free. No learning curve.
  • •Cons: Notoriously underprices properties. Optimises for Airbnb's goals (more bookings) not yours (more revenue).
  • •Verdict: Fine for absolute beginners. You'll likely outgrow it within months.

Option 2: PriceLabs ($19.99/month per listing)

The most popular third-party dynamic pricing tool. Connects to Airbnb, Vrbo, and most channel managers.

  • •Pros: Powerful customisation. Event detection. Competitor analysis.
  • •Cons: Steep learning curve. Requires 5 to 10 hours to set up properly.
  • •Setup time: 5 to 10 hours initially. 2 to 4 hours monthly for monitoring.

Option 3: Wheelhouse ($19.99/month or 1% of booking revenue)

Similar functionality to PriceLabs with a percentage-based model option.

  • •Pros: Can pay percentage instead of flat fee. Good market data.
  • •Cons: 1% adds up quickly on high-earning properties.

Option 4: Beyond Pricing (1% of booking revenue)

Focuses on simplicity with less customisation but easier to use.

Step-by-Step: Setting Up PriceLabs Yourself

  1. 1Create account and connect your Airbnb listing (30 minutes)
  2. 2Set your base price through comparable research (1 to 2 hours)
  3. 3Configure minimum and maximum prices (30 minutes)
  4. 4Set up seasonal adjustments (1 to 2 hours)
  5. 5Add event adjustments for Australian Open, F1, etc. (2 to 3 hours)
  6. 6Configure day-of-week adjustments (30 minutes)
  7. 7Set up last-minute discounts and far-out premiums (1 hour)
  8. 8Review and test (2 to 3 hours)

Total initial setup: 10 to 15 hours if you're thorough.

The Ongoing Time Investment

  • •Weekly price reviews: 30 minutes
  • •Event monitoring: 1 to 2 hours monthly
  • •Competitor watching: 1 hour monthly
  • •Performance analysis: 1 to 2 hours monthly

Total ongoing time: 4 to 8 hours per month to manage properly.

The Hidden Cost

Most hosts set and forget. They spend 10 hours on setup, then rarely revisit settings. The tool underperforms because it's not maintained—you're essentially paying for a service you're not using properly.

The Real Cost Calculation

For a Melbourne property earning $60,000 annually:

  • •Tool cost: $240 per year (PriceLabs)
  • •Initial setup: 12 hours × your hourly value
  • •Ongoing management: 72 hours per year × your hourly value

If your time is worth $50 per hour:

  • •Setup: $600 one-time
  • •Annual management: $3,600
  • •Tool cost: $240
  • •Total first-year cost: $4,440

For a 15 to 25% revenue increase on a $60,000 property, you'd gain $9,000 to $15,000. After subtracting your time investment, the net gain is $5,000 to $11,000.

That's still worthwhile—but there's a catch.

Where DIY Dynamic Pricing Falls Short

  • •Most hosts set and forget. They spend 10 hours on setup, then rarely revisit settings. The tool underperforms because it's not maintained.
  • •Event detection misses local opportunities. Tools know about the Australian Open but might miss the medical conference at MCEC.
  • •Learning curve means lost revenue early on. Your first few months are essentially training—with real money.
  • •No integration with everything else. Pricing is just one piece. You're still handling messages, cleaning, maintenance, reviews.

The Decision Framework

DIY dynamic pricing makes sense if:

  • •You genuinely enjoy optimising pricing
  • •You have 6+ hours monthly to dedicate to it
  • •You're willing to learn the tools properly
  • •Your time is valued under $30/hour

Professional management makes sense if:

  • •Your time is better spent elsewhere
  • •You want pricing handled alongside everything else
  • •You'd rather have predictable, hands-off income
  • •You've tried DIY and found yourself not maintaining it
